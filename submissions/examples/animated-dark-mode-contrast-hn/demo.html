Accessibility (A11y) Fix

Dark Mode Contrast Compliance

Issue Fixed: Previously, headings (h1-h6) were hardcoded to var(--ease-color-neutral-900) (very dark gray), and paragraphs (p) to var(--ease-color-neutral-700). When dark mode was activated, these dark colors remained unchanged against the dark background, causing text to become unreadable.

We resolved this by mapping headings and text to semantic variables that update inside prefers-color-scheme media queries.

Heading level 1 (H1)

Heading level 2 (H2)

This is paragraph text. It is designed to be legible. Under WCAG rules, standard text (under 18pt) must have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 against the background to be readable. Large text (headings) must have a ratio of at least 3:1.

Before Fix (Original Dark Mode)

Heading Contrast: 1.1 : 1 FAIL

Text Contrast: 1.8 : 1 FAIL

Dark gray text rendered on a dark blue background was invisible to users.

After Fix (Updated Dark Mode)

Heading Contrast: 18.2 : 1 PASS (AAA)

Text Contrast: 10.3 : 1 PASS (AAA)

Headings dynamically shift to light gray, maintaining perfect readability.
