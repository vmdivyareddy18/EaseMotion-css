Text Rendering Utilities

Configure optimization models to control font legibility, ligature configurations, drawing speeds, and geometric scaling precision inside browsers.

Text Rendering Modes

Auto Rendering

rendering-auto

Delegates optimization layout to the browser. The browser automatically shifts priorities between drawing speeds and letter rendering dynamically depending on text parameters.

Auto rendering optimizes automatically.

Optimize Speed

rendering-speed

Prioritizes fast paint operations over advanced features. Disables kerning adjustments and typographic ligatures (like "fi", "fl", "->"), suitable for rapidly changing data fields.

Speed: fi fl ffl ffi -> =>

Optimize Legibility

rendering-legibility

Prioritizes reading comfort and typography beauty. Force-enables font kerning tables and standard ligatures. Excellent for titles, paragraphs, and long articles.

Legibility: fi fl ffl ffi -> =>

Geometric Precision

rendering-precision

Prioritizes exact letter rendering precision during font scaling and animations. Prevents character jittering or rounding shifts. Essential for text animations.

Precision scales fonts smoothly.

Font Smoothing & Ligatures

Antialiasing Modes

Antialiasing modes dictate how subpixels are rendered. WebKit browsers show distinct results on high-DPI screens:

font-smooth-subpixel
Subpixel smoothing: cleaner and sharper font weights on non-retina screens.
font-smooth-antialiased
Antialiased smoothing: softer edges, often preferred on dark backgrounds.

Typographic Ligatures

Geometric Precision Scaling Animation

When scaling or moving text, the browser recalculates letter geometries. `geometricPrecision` tells the browser to draw curves cleanly instead of snapping to integer pixel boundaries.
