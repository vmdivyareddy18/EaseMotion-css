Line Height

line-height values and their readability impact

line-height: 1

Cramped text that is hard to read because lines touch each other. This demonstrates why adequate line-height matters for body text.

line-height: 1.2

Slightly better but still tight. Short lines of text feel dense and overwhelming. Not ideal for long-form reading experiences.

line-height: 1.5

The sweet spot for body text. Lines breathe well and readability improves significantly. Most commonly used value.

line-height: 1.8

Generous spacing that works well for accessibility. Some users prefer larger line-height for easier tracking across lines.

line-height: 2

Double spacing. Useful for draft documents or when annotations are needed between lines. Too sparse for dense content.
