136 Years of Excellence

A Legacy Measured in Decades

From a single workshop on the Rue du Rhône to fourteen boutiques across five continents — our story is told in movements, not marketing.

The Geneva Atelier · Est. 1888

The Founding Philosophy

Edouard Chrono founded our maison on a single conviction: that a watch must outlive its owner. Every decision — from movement architecture to case finishing — flows from that belief. We have never outsourced a movement complication and we never will.

1888

Founded in Geneva

Edouard Chrono establishes our atelier with four master watchmakers on the Rue du Rhône, producing twelve timepieces in the first year.

1912

First In-House Calibre

Calibre CE-01 — a 19-ligne pocket watch movement with a 96-hour power reserve — wins the Grand Prix at the International Exhibition in Bern.

1953

The Chronograph Revolution

We introduce the world's first column-wheel vertical-clutch wrist chronograph, setting a benchmark that the industry would spend thirty years trying to equal.

1979

Surviving the Quartz Crisis

While competitors pivot to electronics, we double investment in mechanical mastery. The decision defines us. We emerge as one of only eleven independent Swiss houses still producing fully in-house calibres.

2010

The CE-9015 Skeleton

Three years of development yields our most complex movement: 320 components, double tourbillon, perpetual calendar, and a minute repeater — finished entirely by hand.

2024

The Centennial Reserve

To mark 136 years of independence, we release 88 numbered pieces of the Heritage Perpétuel — our most ambitious timepiece to date, priced from CHF 88,000.

The Atelier

Materials & Craftsmanship

Every material we use is selected for a single reason — longevity. Decades from now, your Chrono Élégance will look as it did the day it left Geneva.

Sapphire Crystal

Double-domed, anti-reflective on both sides. Mohs hardness of 9 — yielding only to diamond. 2.8mm thick to survive any depth rated.

18K Gold Alloy

Proprietary rose gold compound with palladium for scratch resistance. Satin and mirror finishes applied by hand by our Polisseur d'Or.

Grade 5 Titanium

Used in aerospace and surgery. Stronger than steel at 45% the weight. Hypoallergenic, corrosion-proof, and DLC-coated for a decade of flawless wear.

Ceramic Bezel

High-tech zirconia ceramic sintered at 1,500°C. Virtually unscratchable, retains colour after UV decades, and feels cool to the touch in any climate.

Swiss Automatic Movement

CE-9015 calibre. 28,800 vph, 72-hour power reserve. Every rotor, bridge, and wheel decorated by hand: côtes de Genève, bevelled angles, polished screw heads.

Hand-Finished Dial

Argenté silver dials begin as raw brass. Eight weeks of guillochage, lacquering, and applied baton indices — each index bevelled and set under a loupe.

Premium Leather Straps

Hermès alligator, Mississippi crocodile, or hand-stitched calfskin — each strap tanned in our Geneva workshop. Replaces in 90 seconds with our quick-release system.

Water Resistance

All Chrono Élégance references are tested to at least 50m. Diver models certified to 300m under ISO 6425. Every case pressure-tested before shipping.
