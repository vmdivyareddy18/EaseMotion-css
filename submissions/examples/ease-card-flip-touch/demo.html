EaseMotion CSS

ease-card-flip

Touch device verification & fix. The card on the left uses the original hover-only trigger; the card on the right uses a tap-toggled alternative built for touch devices.

Hover Trigger vs. Tap Trigger

On a desktop with a mouse, both cards behave well. On a touch device (or in DevTools touch emulation), hover the left card — notice it either doesn't flip at all, or flips and gets stuck since there's no "mouse leave" event to reverse it. The right card flips reliably on tap, every time, on any device.

🖱️

Hover Trigger

ease-card-flip · :hover only

Mouse Required

Works great with a cursor. Unreliable on touch — no hover-out event to reverse the flip.

hover (desktop)
👆

Tap Trigger

ease-card-flip-tap · tap toggle

Touch Safe

Tap to flip, tap again to flip back. Works identically on mouse click and touch tap.

tap (any device)

Usage

/* 1. Link the stylesheet */
<link rel="stylesheet" href="style.css" />

/* 2a. Desktop / mouse-only contexts: hover trigger is fine */
<div class="flip-card ease-card-flip">
  <div class="flip-card-inner">
    <div class="flip-card-front">Front</div>
    <div class="flip-card-back">Back</div>
  </div>
</div>

/* 2b. Any device (recommended default): tap trigger */
<div class="flip-card ease-card-flip-tap"
     role="button" tabindex="0" aria-pressed="false">
  <div class="flip-card-inner">
    <div class="flip-card-front">Front</div>
    <div class="flip-card-back">Back</div>
  </div>
</div>

/* 3. Tap trigger requires this small JS toggle (see below) */
<script src="card-flip-tap.js"></script>
