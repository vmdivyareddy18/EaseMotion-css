Directional Scroll Progress

Scroll down this page. You will see three distinct types of native CSS scroll progress indicators activating simultaneously without JavaScript.

1. Top Horizontal Bar

Look at the top edge of the screen. A pink progress bar is growing horizontally across the viewport. This is the classic reading progress indicator seen on many blogs, controlled by the `.ease-scroll-progress-top` utility.

2. Right Vertical Bar

Look at the right edge of the screen. A matching pink progress bar is growing vertically downwards. Controlled by the `.ease-scroll-progress-right` utility, this is excellent for side-navigation dashboards or documentation sites.

3. Custom Styling Variables

Look at the bottom right corner of the screen. You'll see a floating action button (FAB) with a green circular progress ring wrapping around it. This is driven by `.ease-scroll-progress-radial` utilizing `@property` to animate a conic gradient.

Furthermore, notice how the ring is green while the bars are pink. This is because the radial indicator overrides the `--ease-scroll-progress-color` variable locally!

Almost at the bottom

As you reach the end of the content, all three progress indicators should seamlessly reach 100% completion simultaneously since they are all bound to `scroll(root block)`.
