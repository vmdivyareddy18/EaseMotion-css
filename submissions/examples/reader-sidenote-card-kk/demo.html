Reader Sidenote Card

Context that stays close to the reading flow.

This demo shows an editorial-style layout where supporting notes live beside the main content instead of interrupting it.

Article excerpt

Design systems work better when guidance is visible in the moment.

Readers move faster when supporting details are nearby. Instead of forcing a jump to the bottom of the page, contextual notes can sit beside the content and offer just enough extra meaning.

This makes long-form interfaces feel more editorial and more intentional. The main content keeps its rhythm, while references, tips, and commentary stay attached to the exact point where they are most useful.

Contextual notes are especially useful for onboarding docs, writing tools, research summaries, and product explainers.

Note

Keep secondary information lightweight

A sidenote works best when it adds context without overwhelming the main content. Short annotations, citations, and quick product tips are ideal.

Reference

This layout can collapse into stacked cards on smaller screens so the note remains visible without requiring a separate overlay or drawer.
