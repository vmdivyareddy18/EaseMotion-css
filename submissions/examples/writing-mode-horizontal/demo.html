writing-mode

horizontal-tb vs vertical-rl vs vertical-lr

Horizontal (horizontal-tb)

Default writing mode — top to bottom, left to right

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. This is the standard horizontal writing mode used by most languages including English.

Line breaks occur naturally and text flows from left to right across the viewport.

Vertical Right-to-Left (vertical-rl)

Text flows vertically from top to bottom, lines stack right to left

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Used for traditional Chinese, Japanese, and Korean text.

Each character stands upright and lines progress from right to left.

Vertical Left-to-Right (vertical-lr)

Text flows vertically from top to bottom, lines stack left to right

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Mongolian script uses this direction.

Lines start on the left and progress to the right, like horizontal text.
