Squash and Stretch
EaseMotion Class: .ease-rubber-band

Gives a sense of weight and flexibility to drawn objects. In UI design, squash and stretch is applied when tabs or buttons are clicked to indicate elastic feedback.

Detailed Motion Theories

      

        

          
Slow In & Slow Out

          

            Objects in the real world require time to accelerate and decelerate.
            Use custom cubic-bezier curves instead of linear transitions to make elements feel natural.
          

        


        

          
Secondary Action

          

            Adding a secondary interaction (like a subtle fade or background scale) supports the primary movement, enriching the user experience without cluttering the screen.
