Isolation Utilities

Control whether elements create a new stacking context to restrict background blending

Active class: .isolation-auto

Currently: Blended with Background. The blending circles are bleeding through the white card and blending with the pink pattern behind it because no new stacking context is created.
Default: isolation-auto

Under isolation: auto, stacking context is not forced. If child elements have blending modes, they will blend with all elements layered behind them, even passing through their immediate parent container to blend with the outermost background.

Usage Case

Useful when you want complex layer blending effects to ripple across all backgrounds in your document.

Isolated: isolation-isolate

Under isolation: isolate, a new stacking context is created. Any child elements with blending modes (like difference, multiply, overlay) are restricted and will only blend with layers inside their isolated parent container.

Usage Case

Crucial for preventing elements (like modal dialog text, complex widget indicators) from bleeding into unrelated body backgrounds or headers behind the widget.
