mix-blend-mode: difference Sweep

A sweeping element with mix-blend-mode: difference inverts all colors beneath it as it passes — no color values touched.

Hover-triggered & Auto-sweep

Hover to sweep

mix-blend-mode: difference inverts every color the sweeper passes over.

Auto sweep

Looping sweep via @keyframes — useful for loading highlights or attention draws.

The sweeper is a ::after pseudo-element with mix-blend-mode: difference. White sweeping over any color produces its visual complement.

Exclusion variant — softer inversion

mix-blend-mode: exclusion

Produces a softer, lower-contrast inversion — desaturates instead of fully complementing.

Swap mix-blend-mode: difference for exclusion to reduce the intensity. Both modes require isolation: isolate on the parent to work correctly.

Why isolation: isolate matters

Without isolation (broken)

No isolation

The blend bleeds to the page background instead of staying inside the card.

With isolation: isolate (correct)

With isolation

The blend stays contained inside the card — inverts card colors only.

isolation: isolate creates a new stacking context that stops mix-blend-mode from compositing against elements below. Always required on the parent of a blend-mode element.
