Our Story

The Art of Making Something Lasting

In 1987, Isabelle Élara opened a small atelier on the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré with a single conviction: that a handbag should be an heirloom, not a season. Each Maison Élara piece is still cut by hand from a single hide, selected by our master maroquiniers for grain consistency and natural character.

The patinated brass hardware is cast in our own foundry in Lyon. The silk linings are woven to our specification in Como. Nothing is sourced from a catalogue. Everything is made to endure.

  • 🤲
    Handmade Process

    Every seam hand-stitched by a single artisan from start to finish.

  • 🏆
    Artisan Expertise

    Our maroquiniers train for seven years before touching a Signature piece.

  • 📜
    Four Decades of Heritage

    Techniques passed from master to apprentice since our founding in Paris.

  • 🌿
    Premium Full-Grain Leather

    Sourced exclusively from certified French and Italian tanneries.

Our Materials

Only the Finest

We accept no compromise in material selection. Every component is chosen for longevity, provenance, and tactile excellence.

🇮🇹

Italian Leather

Full-grain calfskin from the finest Florentine tanneries, selected hide by hide.

🧣

Suede

Buttery nappa suede with a brushed finish that deepens beautifully with age.

✨

Gold Hardware

18ct gold-plated cast brass. Our foundry in Lyon produces every clasp and ring.

🪡

Silk Lining

Jacquard-woven silk from Como, featuring our signature monogram pattern.

🌱

Sustainable Materials

Certified responsible sourcing. Every hide traceable back to its origin farm.

🧵

Hand-Stitched Finish

Linen thread waxed with beeswax. Over 300 saddle stitches per signature piece.
