text-wrap: pretty vs balance

Three side-by-side cards show how each text-wrap value affects multi-line text. Resize the viewport to see the differences in action.

text-wrap: wrap default

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog near the riverbank.

This is the default wrapping behavior. The browser wraps text naturally, line by line, without considering the visual balance of the overall paragraph.

text-wrap: balance headings

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog near the riverbank.

Balance distributes the text evenly across lines, minimizing the rag and creating a more symmetrical appearance. Ideal for headings and short-form content where presentation matters.

text-wrap: pretty readability

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog near the riverbank.

Pretty optimizes the last line to avoid orphaned words (single words on the last line). It improves readability for long-form text without the stricter balancing of the balance value.

When to use each

Value Best for Trade-off
wrap Body text, long articles Can leave uneven rags
balance Headlines, cards, banners Shorter lines on narrow screens
pretty Paragraphs, descriptions Slight performance cost
