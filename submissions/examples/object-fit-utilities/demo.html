Object Fit Utilities

CSS object-fit utilities specifying how images or video media should resize inside their layout boxes. Replaces manual cropping and canvas hacks.

Object Fit Comparisons

Fill .of-fill

Stretches the image to completely fill the 250x180 container. The original aspect ratio is ignored, causing the circle to stretch into an ellipse.

Contain .of-contain

Scales the image proportionally so it fits completely within the container. Maintains aspect ratio, leaving empty letterbox/pillarbox space.

Cover .of-cover

Scales the image proportionally to cover the entire container. Part of the image is cropped to maintain aspect ratio and fill the grid box.

None .of-none

Displays the image at its original 150x150 size without scaling. Parts of the container are left empty, and the image is centered inside.

Scale Down .of-scale-down

Compares the result of none and contain, then applies the one that yields the smaller size. In this case, displays at 150x150.
