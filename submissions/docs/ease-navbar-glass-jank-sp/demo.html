Why sticky + blur hitches

  • backdrop-filter is not free GPU

    Every scroll frame, the browser must sample and blur whatever sits behind the navbar. Busy gradients + looping elements increase that work dramatically.

  • Sticky promotes a persistent overlay Compositor

    ease-navbar-glass-sticky keeps the bar on-screen. That is good UX — and means blur never “rests” while the page moves.

  • Looping utilities never idle Motion

    Classes like ease-bounce, ease-pulse, and ease-ping keep animating under the glass, so the backdrop sample region keeps changing even when you stop scrolling.

  • Solid / translucent is cheaper Alt

    A sticky bar with an opaque or lightly translucent background (no blur) still feels modern and scrolls much smoother.

Safe sticky checklist

  • ✓ Keep sticky if you need it — drop or lower blur ((--ease-navbar-glass-blur) on long/content-heavy pages.
  • ✓ Avoid infinite looping animations directly under the glass bar’s blur region (hero chrome is enough).
  • ✓ Respect prefers-reduced-motion — pause loops and consider disabling blur for that preference.
  • ✓ Prefer translucent solid backgrounds over large backdrop-filter radii (24px+) on mobile.
  • ✕ Don’t stack sticky glass + full-page looping utilities + heavy gradients without testing low-end scroll FPS.
  • ✕ Don’t assume hitch means the navbar class is “broken.” Profile blur + motion cost first.

Copy-paste snippets

Full glass sticky (lab default) vs a safer sticky bar without expensive blur.

Glass sticky (can hitch under busy content) 

<nav class="ease-navbar-glass ease-navbar-glass-sticky ease-navbar-glass-blur">
  <a class="ease-navbar-brand" href="/">Brand</a>
  <div class="ease-navbar-menu">
    <a class="ease-navbar-item" href="#docs">Docs</a>
  </div>
</nav>

Safer sticky (reduce blur / use opaque fallback) 

<nav
  class="ease-navbar-glass ease-navbar-glass-sticky"
  style="--ease-navbar-glass-blur: 0px; background: rgba(255,255,255,0.94);"
>
  <a class="ease-navbar-brand" href="/">Brand</a>
  <div class="ease-navbar-menu">
    <a class="ease-navbar-item" href="#docs">Docs</a>
  </div>
</nav>
